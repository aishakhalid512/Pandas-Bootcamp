Pivoting Table¶

Pivoting is all about Reshaping data

  • We take a DataFrame or Series without changing or modifying the content we present it differently.

For each School , the three sections of the Exam : "Math" , "Reading" and "Writing" are represented in separate records or Rows.

Because of this , certain attribute values are getting repeated , like : "Percent Tested" or the "Student Enrollment" or the "Borough". All of these attributes are repeating within Schools and differ across Schools .

So, we can reasonably argue that this is not the most sensible way to present data.

To avoid this unnecessary duplication in school specific attributes , we might be interested in changing the structure of the DataFrame to the Column axis separately

ValueError: Index contains duplicate entries, cannot reshape

Pandas throws a Value Error here indicatin that the Index contains Duplicate entries. Meaning the Index values will not be Unique. Because each Borough has many schools .If we only use Borough in the index , we will have several repeating records.

As we mentioned before , Indices in Pandas could certainly contain Duplicate Values

For example :¶

We could set Borough as Index on our main dataframe. Pandas will not complain even though the values are repeating in the Index. As we see here. But, this may have some Performance Implications.

But the point is :

Pandas allows Duplicates in the Index of a regular DataFrame¶

However , the Index provided to the Pivot method cannot contain Duplicate Values.

  • Notice that , in the main DataFrame "School Name" also has repeated values
  • If we unstack the "SAT Section" or pivot it to the "column axis" , "School Name" all of a sudden becomes unique , but if we do the same with "Borough" still doesn't become unique because each "Borough" has several schools.

Pivoting the dataset this way , requires some sort of Aggregation.

The Pivot method does not support that

The pivot table¶

Peviously we saw that the "pivot" does not support Aggregation.

If we want to "pivot" using "Borough" in the Index , because each Borough has many schools, we need a way to condense or Aggregate these many categories into a single Borough level result.

To do something like that we turn to the "pivot_table" method

  • pivot_table() just like pivot() has a very convenient interface

The Problem with Average Percentage¶

In [118]:
#School A : 100 students , 30% took the SAT
#School B : 1000  students , 60% took the SAT
In [119]:
1000 * 0.6
Out[119]:
600.0

600 Students School B

In [120]:
100 * 0.3
Out[120]:
30.0

30 Students School A

In [121]:
(0.3 + 0.6) / 2
Out[121]:
0.44999999999999996

If we look at the Average of these percentages :

  • School A : 100 students , 30% took the SAT
  • School B : 1000 students , 60% took the SAT

We end up with concluding that 45% of the students took the exam . But , is it correct? ::: No its NOT

School A : 100 students , 30% took the SAT ===> 30 Students took the test

School B : 1000 students , 60% took the SAT ===> 600 students took the test

In [122]:
#That will be :
(30 + 600) / (100 + 1000)
Out[122]:
0.5727272727272728

It comes to 57%

  • Thats why Averages of Percentages are very tricky and very misleading
